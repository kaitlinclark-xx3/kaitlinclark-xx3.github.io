About Developing A Black Identity

Questions Who would I be?
Without the standardization of the human being,
“Indirectly” molding me into the person I should be

Who should I be?
In a world filled with problems without resolutions
And people with resolutions that don’t foresee an end to the problem.
Who would I be?

To my mother
If I unloaded the heavy burden of my heart and revealed
My deepest dreams and desires
Would I still be the same?
Could I even stand myself?
For if my mother can’t stand the sight of me
How important and worthwhile am I?

Who should I be?
For myself?
Do I owe myself the time and effort of seeing what could be...
If I had done that...
If I had chosen myself over who I should be?

Who would I be without that label?
To shield me from the loneliness and disparity of being my own person,
In spite of my uniform environment
It would be so easy just to…

Who would I be if I resented those who succumbed to the temptation of such a label?
Who am I to judge?
I have also succumbed to the thought.
Am I worthy?

Terrified with the consequence of such an action
Of such a label
Terrified without one.
Who would I be if I just lived alone?
Who should I be in the face of others?

But who I am…
I am different
Whether or not I chose to accept the fact
I am important
For the simple reason that my simple indecision about my course of action liberates others to question theirs as well.
I am change.
I am worthy as all are worthy of such importance
Such a choice as to chose to liberate a world concealed by labels
Such a choice as to chose to let themselves shine for the world
Such importance
As to propose a change
Feared,
Terrified of the consequence of such an action
Such a change
I am smart.
I am strange.
I am queer.
I am like nothing you have ever seen before
I chose to be me.

So?
Who would you be?
Without the standardization of the human being,
“Indirectly” molding you into the person you should be.

Who should you be?
In a world filled with problems without resolutions
And people with resolutions that don’t foresee an end to the problem

Who are you?
Who do you choose to be?
