“We have womyn that swim in deepest oceans of blue black ebony to womyn luminous with the light of the moon, all the womyn in between, the chestnut browns, the deep mahoganies, the almond browns, the honey brown and lustrous golds. Sweet and alluring, wise and discerning, carrying the words and whispers of our ancestors in her womb and her hair, on her lips and on her mind. OH...yes my brother...I know the beauty of my sisters…”

The Black Girls (TBG]. (Eds.). (1995). Black girl talk. Toronto, Ontario: Sister Vision Press.
